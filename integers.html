Understanding Integers

What are Integers?

Integers are whole numbers that can be positive, negative, or zero. They do not have any fractional or decimal parts.

Examples of Integers:

  • Positive Integers: 1, 2, 100, 1000
  • Negative Integers: -1, -50, -100
  • Zero: 0

Representing Integers on a Number Line:

An integer can be represented on a number line. Positive integers are to the right of zero, and negative integers are to the left of zero.

Operations with Integers:

Integers can be added, subtracted, multiplied, and divided just like other numbers.

  • Addition: 5 + (-3) = 2
  • Subtraction: 10 - 8 = 2
  • Multiplication: (-4) * 3 = -12
  • Division: 20 / (-5) = -4

Summary:

Integers are whole numbers that include positive numbers, negative numbers, and zero. They are used in various mathematical operations and can be represented on a number line.

Understanding Fractions

Fractions represent parts of a whole. They consist of a numerator (top number) and a denominator (bottom number).

Examples of Fractions:

  • Proper Fractions: 1/2, 3/4, 5/6
  • Improper Fractions: 7/4, 10/3, 15/2
  • Mixed Numbers: 1 1/2, 2 3/4, 3 2/5

Representing Fractions:

Fractions can be represented on a number line or in visual diagrams to depict the portion of a whole.

Operations with Fractions:

Fractions can be added, subtracted, multiplied, and divided by following specific rules for each operation.

  • Addition: 1/3 + 1/4 = 7/12
  • Subtraction: 5/6 - 2/3 = 1/6
  • Multiplication: 2/5 * 3/4 = 6/20 (or simplified to 3/10)
  • Division: 3/4 / 1/2 = 3/2 (or simplified to 1 1/2)

Summary:

Fractions represent parts of a whole and can be proper fractions, improper fractions, or mixed numbers. They are used in various mathematical operations and can be represented visually.
