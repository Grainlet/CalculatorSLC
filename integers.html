Understanding Integers

What are Integers?

Integers are whole numbers that can be positive, negative, or zero. They do not have any fractional or decimal parts.

Examples of Integers:

  • Positive Integers: 1, 2, 100, 1000
  • Negative Integers: -1, -50, -100
  • Zero: 0

Representing Integers on a Number Line:

An integer can be represented on a number line. Positive integers are to the right of zero, and negative integers are to the left of zero.

Operations with Integers:

Integers can be added, subtracted, multiplied, and divided just like other numbers.

  • Addition: 5 + (-3) = 2
  • Subtraction: 10 - 8 = 2
  • Multiplication: (-4) * 3 = -12
  • Division: 20 / (-5) = -4

Summary:

Integers are whole numbers that include positive numbers, negative numbers, and zero. They are used in various mathematical operations and can be represented on a number line.
